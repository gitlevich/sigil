# SemanticCoherence

How well each sigil knows what it is, measured in embedding space.

Each sigil name, its domain language, and its affordances should land in a tight region of meaning. Internal scatter means the sigil is confused about what it is. Overlapping neighbor centroids mean the boundary between them is unclear. A contained sigil whose centroid drifts far from its container may be in the wrong place.

The partner asks the author to describe what a sigil name means precisely because it needs that description to assess coherence. The act of answering sharpens the design. The metric is a byproduct of the conversation.
