# Experience

An experience is a grouping of affordances in the narration — what it is like to use a coherent part of the application. Experiences are the contained sigils of the application sigil at the UX level of narration. Up to five experiences per application.

An experience is not a feature list. It is a narrative of use: what the user reaches for, what feels effortless, what the application does for them in this context. The affordances within it are what the user can do while inhabiting this experience.

Experiences are spoken into existence by narrating use. As I describe what I am doing, the experiences emerge as natural groupings of action. Their names are the names of the contained sigils. Their affordances are what I declare within them.

The domain model spoken at the next level of narration must be sufficient to deliver every experience and every affordance declared here.
