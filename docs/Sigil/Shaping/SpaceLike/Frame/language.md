# Frame

A relationship is a declared dependency between neighbors — a statement that one sigil's trajectory references or relies on another's affordances. Without a declared relationship, neighbors are separate: their trajectories do not interact.

A relationship has direction. The sigil that depends on the affordance is downstream; the sigil that exposes it is upstream. A symmetric relationship means both depend on each other. Direction matters because it constrains how change propagates: when an upstream trajectory shifts, every downstream trajectory that references it is a candidate for review.

Undeclared dependencies are the structural equivalent of accidental gaps in @Contrasts — the trajectories interact, but the interaction is unnamed and invisible until something breaks.
