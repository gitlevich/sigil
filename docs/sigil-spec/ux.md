# UX

I use Sigil to speak a system into existence before writing code. I open a sigil and I am immediately inside a context. The context is mine to define.

I work across three experiences: @Shaping, @Navigation, and @Distillation. Together they cover everything I do here.

---

**Shaping** — I write. I open a sigil and the UX panel is active. I narrate what the user reaches for in this context. As I write, sigil names appear as @references — highlighted, navigable. When I name something I Cmd+click it to enter it one level down, where I elaborate it further. Alt+Enter on an unresolved @reference creates the sigil it names. Alt+Cmd+R renames a sigil across all references. I move between panels — UX, Language, Architecture, Implementation — as the spec deepens. I cannot contradict what I declared above.

**Navigation** — I move. The tree on the left shows where I am and lets me jump anywhere quickly. The map shows the structure of the current sigil spatially — contained sigils as named cells, deeper structure as anonymous texture. Focused view shows one level at a time. Revealed view shows full recursive depth. Both views reflect the same position and stay in sync.

**Distillation** — I talk. The chat panel on the right is my AI partner. It inhabits the entire sigil — every panel at every level. It detects drift, misplaced concepts, boundary violations. It can write language, create sigils, rename, delete. It measures coherence in embedding space and tells me where the membrane is open. I can have multiple conversations per sigil. I can invite a human collaborator into a three-way conversation.
