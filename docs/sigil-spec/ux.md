# UX

I use Sigil to speak a system into existence before writing code. I open a sigil and I am immediately inside a trajectory. The structure of what I have already said is around me. I continue from here.

I work across three experiences: @Shaping, @Navigation, and @Distillation. Together they cover everything I do here.

**@Shaping** — I narrate. I add direction to trajectories by writing language, declaring invariants, naming affordances. Each thing I write is an edit. Edits cluster into bursts. The burst history is the time-like record of how the sigil grew.

**@Navigation** — I move. I traverse the trajectory tree, descending into contained sigils or ascending to the containing one. The Tree gives me a list view of the structure. The Map gives me the spatial view: where things are, how they relate, how dense each region is.

**@Distillation** — I refine. I work with the @DesignPartner to improve precision and resolution. The partner inhabits the sigil and responds in the domain language. We identify trajectories that are thin, gaps where direction was lost, contradictions between levels. @SigilCoherence tracks the quality of the structure over time.
