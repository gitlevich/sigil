# Lull

A lull is the pause between @Bursts — the gap in editing that marks a @Burst boundary. It is not an absence of activity: it is the signal that attention has shifted, that a focused session of narration has ended and the next has not yet begun.

The lull threshold is not fixed. It is derived from the actual distribution of inter-edit gaps at each sigil. Gaps within a burst are short — seconds or minutes. Gaps between bursts are qualitatively longer. The distribution is bimodal: a short-gap cluster and a long-gap cluster. The lull threshold is the valley between them.

When a gap exceeds the lull threshold, a burst boundary is placed. The edits before the threshold belong to one burst; those after belong to the next.

The threshold is re-derived as the Edit history grows. Early in a sigil's life, with few edits, the threshold is coarse. As the history accumulates, the bimodal structure becomes clearer and the threshold stabilizes.
