# Experience

An experience is a grouping of affordances in the UX panel — what it is like to use a coherent part of the application. Experiences are the contained sigils of the application sigil at the UX level. Up to five experiences per application, following the containment limit.

An experience is not a feature list. It is a narrative of use: what the user reaches for, what feels effortless, what the application does for them in this context. The affordances within it are what the user can do while inhabiting this experience.

Experiences are spoken into existence in the UX panel by narrating use. As I describe what I am doing, the experiences emerge as natural groupings of action. Their names are the names of the contained sigils. Their affordances are what I declare within them.

The Language panel then speaks the domain model into existence such that it can deliver every experience and every affordance declared in UX.
