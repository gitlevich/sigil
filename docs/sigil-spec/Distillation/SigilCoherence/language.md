# SigilCoherence

The quality of a sigil's boundary in embedding space. A coherent sigil has a well-defined membrane — a surface that clearly separates what belongs to this sigil from what does not.

The membrane is not drawn directly. It emerges from the language. The sigil's narration and its affordances, embedded with their full ancestor context, create points in the space. The membrane is the boundary that encloses those points and separates them from neighboring sigils.

Smoothing is the iterative process of tightening the membrane. When an implementing agent produces something unexpected, the author has discovered an undeclared @Contrasts — an axis they care about but had not named. Adding the invariant tightens the membrane in that direction. The process continues until the agent converges on implementations the author accepts.

A smooth membrane is not necessarily dense. Some regions are richly specified — many nested affordances, high resolution. Others are intentionally flat: the author has declared their invariants, and this region is orthogonal to all of them. A flat region is not a defect. An accidental gap on a declared axis is.

@SemanticCoherence measures how well the embedding reflects the membrane. @StructuralComplexity measures how tangled the sigil graph is. @Contrasts is the unit of specification — a declared axis with a preference range.
