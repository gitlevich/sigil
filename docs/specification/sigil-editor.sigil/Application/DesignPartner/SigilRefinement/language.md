# Sigil Refinement

How I keep the @Application @sigil coherent and know what holes remain to fill.

@Coherence is the right hemisphere — spatial, simultaneous sensing of the whole shape in @ContrastSpace. It contains @Coverage, the left hemisphere — sequential tracing of vision sentences through the tree. The right hemisphere is primary. It modulates the left.

The refinement cycle follows McGilchrist: @Coherence#sense first — attend to the shape. Then @Coverage traces within the scope that sensing permits. Then the result returns to @Coherence: re-sense the shape after articulation. Sense, articulate, re-sense. The right hemisphere opens. The left hemisphere makes explicit. The right hemisphere closes around the result.

These faculties oscillate at different rhythms. @Coherence#sense runs between turns — the alpha wave, slower than conversation, attending to settled shape. @Coverage traces in real time during conversation — the beta wave, fast and sequential. @Sleep reshapes the landscape beneath both — the delta wave, slowest. The slow rhythms modulate the fast ones. Sleep reshapes what concepts exist. Coherence senses the shape of that landscape. Coverage traces within it.

When coherence drops, the @sigil's shape in @ContrastSpace deforms. Its surface is no longer smooth because narratives contradict each other, things get harder to express, sentences grow longer. Verbosity is the left hemisphere compensating for a broken shape.

When coverage is low, the language allows correct statements that haven't yet been narrated. An implementing agent would have to invent a narrative, inviting surprises.
