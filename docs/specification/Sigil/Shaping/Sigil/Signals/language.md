# Signals

Signals are the dimensions I explicitly care about, each with a preferred range. A signal is a dimension of attention — I name it because I have a stake in it. Everything I do not name stays in superposition: free for the implementing agent to resolve as it sees fit.

Declaring a signal collapses that dimension in the trajectory. Not declaring it leaves it open — not as a gap, but as a free direction the trajectory simply did not specify.

Signals are discovered iteratively. When an implementation produces something unexpected, I have found a hidden signal: a dimension I was implicitly attending to but had not named. I name it, state my preferred range, and the trajectory tightens in that direction.

An accidental gap is a dimension I am building on — referencing a concept, depending on it — without having declared a preference. The trajectory passes through that dimension but has no direction there. The implementing agent picks an arbitrary direction and I am surprised.
