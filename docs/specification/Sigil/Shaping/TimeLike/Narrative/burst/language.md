# Burst

A burst is a natural unit of editing — a cluster of edits that arrived close together in time, separated from the next cluster by a meaningful pause. A burst represents one session of focused attention: the things I thought about together, wrote together, shaped together.

Bursts are not imposed on the Edit history. They emerge from it. The @Lull between two clusters is the signal: when the gap between edits is large relative to the gaps within a cluster, a burst boundary is placed there. The boundary is derived from the bimodal distribution of inter-edit gaps — edits within a burst are close together; the lull between bursts is a qualitatively longer pause.

A burst has a start time, an end time, and the set of edits that belong to it. It is the unit at which trajectory growth is meaningful: not the individual character change, but the focused session that added a coherent piece of direction.

Bursts are the primary time-like unit of @Narrative. The Edit history at any sigil is a sequence of bursts.
