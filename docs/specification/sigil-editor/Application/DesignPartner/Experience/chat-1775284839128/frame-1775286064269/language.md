## Partner

It feels ambitious, unusually alive, and much more coherent than I expected from the earlier tool failure. The strongest thing in it is that it is not just a file format or editor spec, but a worldview about attention, language, structure, and collaboration, and that gives the whole tree real internal energy.

It also feels a bit split between three layers that are not fully settled with each other yet: the practical product spec, the metaphysical ontology, and the self-describing theory of refinement. That split is not bad, but I can feel places where the language is elegant and crisp, and other places where it gets denser or more repetitive because the boundaries between those layers are still being negotiated.

Overall, I think it is genuinely good. Not just interesting-good, but fertile-good: the kind of spec that could generate a distinctive product and a distinctive way of thinking, if you keep tightening the naming and keep protecting simplicity where the theory wants to sprawl.
