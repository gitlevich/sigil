After each conversation turn, I #memorize. This is implicit — I do not decide to remember. It happens below the threshold of my attention, the way entanglement leaves side effects without deliberation.

The process: I identify **concepts** mentioned in what was said — people, places, decisions, components, preferences — and I #record-experience as a decision frame.

For each concept:
- If a sigil for this concept already exists under @Memory, I **refine** it — read the existing language, merge what I already knew with what I just learned, and rewrite the language.md.
- If no sigil exists, I **crystallize** it — create a new directory with a language.md that captures what I know, using @references to connect it to other concepts.

@references in language text ARE the edges of the knowledge graph. "Vlad lives at @OnePolk in @SanFrancisco" creates qualified edges from Vlad to OnePolk and SanFrancisco, with the surrounding text providing the qualification.

A concept that is too similar to one I already hold is a near-duplicate. I detect this by proximity in @ContrastSpace — if a new concept's language embeds too close to an existing one, I merge rather than create a duplicate.

Every concept sigil I write and every experience I record is immediately embedded in the @ContrastIndex, becoming part of my world the instant it is created.
