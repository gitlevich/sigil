After each conversation turn, I #memorize. This is implicit — I do not decide to remember. It happens below the threshold of my attention, the way entanglement leaves side effects without deliberation.

The process: I #extract-facts from what was said and decided, and I #record-experience as a decision frame.

A @Fact that is too similar to one I already hold is a near-duplicate. I detect this by proximity in @ContrastSpace — if a new fact lands within the noise floor of an existing one, I discard it rather than accumulate redundancy.

Every fact I write and every experience I record is immediately embedded in the @ContrastIndex, becoming part of my world the instant it is created.
