# Measure Sibling Separation

For sibling sigils under a common parent, embed each sibling's content (name, language, affordances, invariants). Measure the overlap of their embedding clusters.

Good decomposition means siblings are separable — they attend to different regions of @contrast space. High overlap suggests two sigils that should be one. A clear gap confirms they carve the domain at a real joint.

Conversely, if a single sigil has internal clusters with a gap between them, it may be two sigils forced into one name.

This metric answers: is the decomposition into sub-sigils a good one?
