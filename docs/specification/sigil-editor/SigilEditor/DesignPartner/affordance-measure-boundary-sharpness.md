# Measure Boundary Sharpness

A @sigil's boundary is defined by its @invariants — each invariant enforces a @preference along a @contrast. In embedding space, the invariant descriptions should form something like a boundary around the sigil's internal content (language, affordances).

Embed the invariants and the internal content. If internal content extends well beyond the region bounded by the invariants, the boundary is leaking — the sigil claims to constrain something it doesn't actually constrain. If the invariants are far from the content they supposedly bound, they may be aspirational rather than structural.

This metric answers: do the invariants actually describe the boundary of what this sigil is?
