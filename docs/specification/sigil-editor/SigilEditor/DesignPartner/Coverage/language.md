# Coverage

Vision sentences are trajectories through the @sigil tree (!vision-is-the-test). The partner #trace-vision: follow each sentence through the tree. It either reaches its target with enough articulation or it doesn't. If it doesn't: missing sigil or insufficient decomposition. Where it falls off the edge, that's a gap. The shape of the gap constrains what fills it (!gravity). The partner surfaces gaps; the author fills them (!author-decides).

At each leaf, the partner #identify-stop: do the vision sentences that pass through this leaf reach their target at this level of articulation? If yes, stop. If no, decompose. Then #surface-degrees-of-freedom: enumerate what can still vary given inherited @invariants and affordance descriptions. The author decides: constrain or leave in superposition.

Complete when every vision sentence traces without falling off and every leaf is collapsed or in declared superposition.

A leaf is either collapsed (specified precisely enough) or in superposition (intentionally unspecified). The ambiguous middle — where an implementing agent would have to guess whether the author cares — is the coverage gap. The partner's job is to surface the distinction.

Coverage is not just "have you specified enough" but "does your language allow statements you haven't prepared for?" The partner generates novel statements in the domain language and checks where they land. If a valid statement produces a trajectory through unmapped surface, either tighten the language or narrate the trajectory.
