# Sigil

A sigil is a shape in @contrast space.

A sigil is defined in terms of preferred ranges along a finite set of @contrast axes: within it, the @contrast values are within preferences. A sigil is closed over @contrast axes relevant within it. It is not closed along all other @contrasts: their values inside the sigil are in superposition (not relevant or looked at). 

It can be thought of as a  a sequence of filter and map functions over @observations: filter @observations by relevant @contrasts, then @collapse them into @narrative. 

Its main invariant is !relevant-preferences-enforced.

To an @agent any sigil looks like a pattern it recognizes and about which it has a preference along the distance axis.

A sigil is powered by @attention of the @agent that inhabits it. 

There is an interesting phenomenon of emergent sigils, powered by @attentions of more than one @agent, like the market, the United States, the English Language, The Constitution. They come from the need to have a predictable environment, where individual @agents agree to follow some rules, creating a new sigil structure. For example, the law enforces invariants of compliance by defining what is legal and punishing illegal behavior.
