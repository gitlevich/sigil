# Contrast

A contrast is the range in contrast space between polar opposites, e.g. dark vs bright. Or less vs more. 

All my senses report to me observations along a large number of contrasts: brightness, color, sound, taste, size. They aggregate into contrasts like good vs bad. Good is what's sitting where I prefer along contrasts I care about. 

The good vs bad contrast is an example of an aggregated contrast: weaved of many layers of more and more primitive contrasts. 

I think contrast is the same thing as "dimension" in ML. 

I prefer contrast to dimension because I can't imagine an embedding space with more than 3 dimensions. 

I don't have to imagine a space with a lot of contrasts: I live in it.

The less vs more contrasts are a different category. They are about a preference for more or less of something. Or of someone. See @entanglement.
